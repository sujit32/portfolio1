My Thesis

A descriptive study was conducted to assess the knowledge regarding lifestyle modification for improving the quality of life in chronic renal failure patients undergoing hemodialysis at GIMSR Hospital, Visakhapatnam. The tools used for data collection included demographic and knowledge assessment instruments. Researchers selected 40 samples for their study and collected data using a structured questionnaire related to knowledge assessment. The study findings indicated that the majority of the patients, 47.5%, were in the age group above 51 years. Additionally, 47.5% were male, 72.5% were Hindu, 65% were employed, and 42.5% were educated. The pretest results showed that 47.5% of patients had average knowledge, while 52.5% had poor knowledge. After providing the self-instruction module, the majority of patients (80%) demonstrated excellent knowledge. Researchers identified the effectiveness of the self-instructional module through an unpaired t-test, which yielded a value of 21.23 at the 0.05 significance level. The study results also indicated that knowledge assessment scores were dependent on selected demographic variables.
